Random Fourier Features, Part I

RFFs deserve to be your method-of-choice for baselines

 Posted on Mar 23, 2024  |  9 minutes  |  Mark Kurzeja

Random Fourier Features, in my opinion, have one of the best performance-to-cost tradeoffs in machine learning techniques today. Simple to code, cheap to fit, and unreasonably effective, they have been my bread-and-butter for small-to-medium learning tasks and serve as an amazing baseline for more complex systems. This post will be the first of a three part series.
